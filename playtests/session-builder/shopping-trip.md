# THE SHOPPING TRIP — Phase 2 Template

## A Mini-Adventure for Gearing Up and Team Bonding

---

## WHAT THIS IS

The Shopping Trip is the bridge between recruiting and the big show. The team has been assembled. Now they need to prepare — acquire equipment, gather intelligence, test each other, and discover what kind of team they actually are before the stakes get real.

This phase is lower-pressure than recruiting (no one's getting cut) and lower-stakes than the big show (no one's going to die). But it's character-revealing in ways the other phases aren't, because the team is operating without the pressure of evaluation or survival. Who they are when they're just... shopping... tells you who they'll be when it matters.

This file is usable standalone or as part of a multi-phase session generated by `session-builder.md`.

---

## INPUTS

These values come from the Session Builder or from the output of `recruiting.md`. If running standalone, generate or specify them directly.

```
TEAM:               [character cards from recruiting phase, or generate/specify fresh]
OPERATOR:           [same operator from recruiting, or None]
MISSION_BRIEF:      [what they're preparing for — needed to know what to shop for]

BEAT_COUNT:         [10-15 — default: 12]
TONE:               [inherited from session or specified]
ARTIFACTS_AVAILABLE:[generated / pre-selected / from prop house]
RELATIONSHIPS:      [carry forward from recruiting if available, otherwise start fresh]
```

## STEP 1 — ESTABLISH THE TEAM

If this follows a recruiting phase, import the team as-is:
- Character cards with any condition changes from recruiting
- Relationship tables (Affinity/Aversion scores carry forward)
- The operator's assessment of each team member
- Any hooks or unresolved tensions from recruiting

If this is standalone, generate or accept CREW_SIZE characters using the same rules as `recruiting.md` Step 2. Generate relationships at baseline (all zeros) or with light pre-existing connections if specified.

---

## STEP 2 — BUILD THE SETTING

The shopping trip setting should feel like a place between — not the safety of home, not the danger of the mission, but the threshold. It's where you go to get ready.

If a setting is provided, use it. Otherwise, generate one from `tables/settings.md` (Shopping Trip section) or invent one that fits the tone.

The setting should have:
- **A marketplace or resource hub** — somewhere to acquire artifacts, information, or services
- **NPCs who sell, trade, or know things** — the Bindery for artifacts, the Dockhands for transit, the Lamplighters for intel, or independent merchants
- **Atmosphere** — sensory details, mood, the feeling of being in a place that exists for people like them
- **At least one thing that isn't for sale** — something the team notices that hints at the mission's true difficulty, or the world's true strangeness

---

## STEP 3 — GENERATE SHOPPING TRIP CONTENT

### Available Goods and Services

Generate 6-10 items, services, or opportunities available at the setting. These should be a mix of:

**Artifacts** (objects with mechanical weight):
- Each artifact needs: Name, Description, Provenance (where it came from), Binding Effect (what happens when a character bonds with it), Cost (not always money — what do they trade?)
- At least one artifact should be clearly useful for the upcoming mission
- At least one should be tempting but risky — powerful with a downside
- At least one should be a curiosity with no obvious use (it might matter later, or it might just be interesting)

**Services** (things NPCs can do for the team):
- Intelligence about the target book or the opposition
- Transit arrangements (a Dockhand who knows a route)
- Repairs or modifications to existing equipment
- Training or preparation specific to the mission environment

**Encounters** (things that happen whether the team wants them or not):
- A rumor about the mission that's partly true and partly wrong
- A character from their past (or from the target book) who appears unexpectedly
- A test of the team dynamic — a situation that forces them to act together before the mission

### NPCs

Generate 2-4 NPCs who inhabit the shopping trip setting. Each gets a mini-profile:

```
**[NAME]** — [Role/Organization]. [One sentence: who they are, what they sell or know, what they want in return.]
```

At least one NPC should have an agenda beyond commerce — they're curious about the mission, they have a warning, they want something the team doesn't expect. NPCs can be drawn from the Prop House if available.

---

## STEP 4 — RUN THE ENCOUNTER

Run BEAT_COUNT beats. The shopping trip has a different rhythm than recruiting — it's episodic rather than escalating. Things happen, but they don't build to a single climax.

### Pacing Structure

| Beats | Phase | What Happens |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1-20% | **Arrival** | Team arrives at the setting. First impressions. The operator (if present) has an agenda for what they need to acquire. |
| 20-50% | **Shopping** | Characters explore, interact with NPCs, acquire or consider items. Character dynamics emerge through choices — who wants what, who's cautious vs. reckless, who haggles and who overpays. |
| 50-75% | **The Complication** | Something disrupts the shopping. A rumor, an encounter, a revelation, a test. The team has to respond together. |
| 75-90% | **Resolution** | The complication resolves. The team's dynamic is clearer — to them and to the reader. |
| 90-100% | **Departure** | Team leaves with what they have. The operator (if present) assesses readiness. Transition to the big show. |

### Character Behavior Rules

Same as recruiting — stats, traits, conditions, filters, and reputations drive behavior AT ALL TIMES.

Additional shopping trip behaviors:
- **What a character chooses to buy reveals character.** A warrior who buys a book. A scholar who buys a weapon. A coward who buys courage in a bottle. The choices are narrative, not just mechanical.
- **Haggling and negotiation use roll skills.** Active skills fire when characters try to negotiate. Passive skills fire when triggered by NPC interactions.
- **Filters affect NPCs.** A character whose filter makes people remember their losses will get different prices, different information, different treatment.
- **Team dynamics from recruiting carry forward.** Characters who bonded during recruiting gravitate together. Characters who clashed avoid each other — or force confrontation in the lower-pressure setting.

### Beat-Close Rules

Same dual-track as recruiting:

**Track 1 — Relationships:**
- Update Affinity/Aversion for all pairs who interacted
- Shopping creates different dynamics than evaluation — bonding over shared interests, conflict over resources, new information about each other

**Track 2 — Readiness Assessment** (replaces operator's recruitment assessment):
- After each beat, assess team readiness for the mission:
  - What have they acquired?
  - What do they still need?
  - What did they learn about the mission?
  - How is the team dynamic? Are they cohering or fracturing?

### Narrative Rules

- Write in present tense.
- The shopping trip should feel lived-in. These are characters between crises. How they handle downtime reveals different facets than how they handle pressure.
- NPCs have their own lives. They're not just vendors — they have opinions about the mission, the team, and the state of the literary web.
- At least one moment of genuine connection between characters that isn't about the mission. They're people, not just operatives.
- At least one moment of tension that isn't about the mission. Resource scarcity, personality friction, a secret surfacing.

### Sympathy Bias Guard

The shopping trip is the most vulnerable phase for premature bonding. The low stakes and shared purpose make the LLM default to team cohesion. Resist this. Some team members don't gel. Some actively grate on each other. Some bond with NPCs more than with their own team. The shopping trip should reveal friction as much as connection.

---

## STEP 5 — OUTPUT

Produce one markdown file containing:

1. **Configuration Summary** — team composition, setting, what was available
2. **Setting Description** — the shopping trip location in full sensory detail
3. **Available Goods & Services** — the full inventory
4. **NPC Profiles** — all shopping trip NPCs
5. **The Encounter** — all beats with narrative and beat-close evaluations
6. **Acquisition Log** — what each character acquired, traded, or learned
7. **Final Relationship Tables** — updated from recruiting baselines
8. **Readiness Assessment** — how prepared is the team for the big show?
9. **Post-Encounter Notes:**
   - What surprised the simulation
   - Character dynamics that shifted during the shopping trip
   - Information the team learned (and didn't learn) about the mission
   - Hooks for the big show
10. **Handoff** — what carries into the big show: team state, acquired equipment, relationship updates, intelligence gathered, unresolved tensions
