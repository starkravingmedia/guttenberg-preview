# RECRUITING — Phase 1 Template

## A Generalized Team Assembly Encounter

---

## WHAT THIS IS

This template generates a complete recruiting encounter where an Operator evaluates and selects a team from a pool of literary characters. It generalizes the patterns proven in the Wednesday Group (Ginsfeld) and Tuesday Group (Kade) engines into a configurable format.

This file is usable standalone (paste it into an LLM) or as part of a multi-phase session generated by `session-builder.md`.

---

## INPUTS

These values are provided by the Session Builder or specified directly by the user. If a value is missing, generate it randomly.

```
OPERATOR:           [Ginsfeld / Kade / Custom / None / Random]
CHARACTER_COUNT:    [number of candidates to generate — default: 4]
CREW_SIZE:          [number to recruit — default: 3]
POWER_LEVEL:        [Noob / Veteran / Mixed / Asymmetric]
ERA_GENRE:          [All literature / Era-locked / Genre-locked / Mix with bias]
UNWRITTEN_ALLOWED:  [No / Yes-one / Yes-any / All-Unwritten]
BEAT_COUNT:         [10 / 20 / 30 / custom — default: 20]
MISSION_TARGET:     [from tables or custom — needed if operator reveals mission during recruiting]
OPPOSITION:         [from tables or custom]
COMPLICATION:       [from tables or custom]
INFO_ASYMMETRY:     [from tables or custom]
SETTING:            [from tables or custom or generate fresh]
TONE:               [Literary / Gothic / Comedic / Thriller / Absurdist / Mixed]
```

### If None:
No operator. The narrator sets the scene and the characters interact without an evaluator. Skip operator-specific beats (reveal, assessment, cut). The session becomes a pure social encounter with a mission briefing delivered by other means (a letter, a message, an artifact, a stranger).

### If Random:
Generate a new operator from scratch. Give them a distinctive recruitment philosophy that is neither Ginsfeld (intimacy) nor Kade (spectacle). Possible angles: recruitment through competition, through shared labor, through crisis, through deception, through pilgrimage, through art. The method should feel like it belongs to a specific person.

---

## STEP 2 — GENERATE CHARACTERS

Generate CHARACTER_COUNT literary characters following these rules:

### Selection Rules
- Draw from the ERA_GENRE setting. If "all literature," slight preference for classics — at least half from texts written before 1900.
- Do NOT select characters to fit the mission. Roll or choose blindly.
- Avoid the most obvious picks (Sherlock Holmes, Odysseus, Hamlet). Go deeper. Use world literature.
- Mix archetypes. Don't generate four warriors or four intellectuals.
- At least one character should be someone most readers would not immediately recognize.
- If UNWRITTEN_ALLOWED, generate Unwritten characters as specified. Unwritten characters have incomplete stats (some scores are "??"), contradictory conditions, and filters that flicker or change. Their descriptions should feel fragmentary — sentences that trail off, details that contradict.

### Power Level Adjustments
- **Noob:** Standard array 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 16. Fewer conditions (2). Simple roll skills. No reputations.
- **Veteran:** Standard array 10, 12, 14, 16, 18, 20. Rich conditions (4-5). Complex roll skills with layered costs. Multiple reputations.
- **Mixed:** Standard array 8, 10, 12, 14, 16, 18. Normal conditions (3-4). Standard roll skills.
- **Asymmetric:** One character gets the veteran array. The rest get the noob array. Or vice versa.

### Character Card Format
Use the full Ginsfeld-era character card format (identical to the wednesday_group_engine template):

- Name + Actor designation + Team affiliation
- Tweet-length bio
- Stats table with modifiers
- Game Traits table
- 3-4 Conditions (with Duration, Visibility, Observability, Description)
- 1-2 Reputations (with Quote name, Source, Description)
- 2 Roll Skills (with Name, Trigger, Mechanic, Flavor — at least one with a cost on success)
- 1 Filter (world instruction, not character instruction)
- Relationships (empty — populated during simulation)
- Equipment (Artifact: unbound, Idea: unbound)

---

## STEP 3 — GENERATE THE MISSION

If mission parameters are provided in the inputs, use them. Otherwise, roll randomly from the tables in `tables/`.

Combine rolls into a mission brief. If an operator is present, write the brief into their card under Mission Parameters. The operator knows this information but manages its revelation according to their recruitment philosophy.

---

## STEP 4 — BUILD THE SETTING

If a setting is provided in the inputs, use it. Otherwise, generate one that fits the operator's style and the session's tone.

The setting should be:
- A specific physical space with sensory details (sight, sound, smell, texture)
- Tonally aligned with the operator (Ginsfeld = institutional intimacy, Kade = industrial spectacle, custom = derived from their personality)
- A character in its own right — the space should exert narrative pressure on the people in it

Write the setting as a short paragraph with enough detail to anchor the scene.

---

## STEP 5 — RUN THE ENCOUNTER

Run BEAT_COUNT beats. Each beat is one unit of narrative action.

### Pacing Structure

Scale the pacing proportionally to the beat count. For a 20-beat session:

| Beats | Phase | What Happens |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1-25% | **Arrivals** | Characters enter. First impressions form. The operator (if present) observes. |
| 25-40% | **Opening** | The cover scenario plays out. Characters reveal themselves through the format. |
| 40-60% | **Cross-connections** | Characters interact with each other, not just the operator. Alliances, friction, chemistry emerge. |
| 60-75% | **The Reveal** | The real purpose surfaces. High Game Savvy characters figure it out early. The mission is disclosed (if applicable). |
| 75-90% | **The Cut** | CHARACTER_COUNT becomes CREW_SIZE. The tension of elimination plays out. |
| 90-100% | **Resolution** | Team forms. Unchosen characters exit. Hooks are left. |

If there is no operator, replace "The Reveal" with the mission arriving by other means, and replace "The Cut" with characters self-selecting or being eliminated by circumstance.

### Character Behavior Rules

- Characters behave according to their stats, traits, conditions, filters, and reputations AT ALL TIMES.
- **Verbosity** determines how much they talk. Low = few words. High = monologues.
- **Honesty** determines default truthfulness. Low = spin, omit, reframe. High = can't help telling the truth.
- **Game Savvy** determines when they figure out hidden mechanics. High = early. Low = late or never.
- **Sensitivity** determines how they read other characters. High = notices emotional shifts. Low = misses them.
- **Filters** modify how others react — honor filters in EVERY interaction.
- Conditions: o2o = visible to everyone. Private = hidden. Semi-private = emerges under pressure.

### Roll Skills

- Passive skills fire automatically when trigger conditions are met. Roll d20 + modifier.
- Active skills fire when the character would reasonably choose to use them.
- Record all rolls in the beat log.
- Let the dice create surprises. Failed rolls are more interesting than successful ones.

### Beat-Close Rules (EVERY BEAT)

After each beat, evaluate all character pairs who interacted:

- Assign 0-3 points of **Affinity** (positive interaction quality)
- Assign 0-3 points of **Aversion** (negative interaction quality)
- Account for conditions, filters, reputations, and skill results
- Write a SHORT note from the character's perspective
- Update running totals

If an operator is present, include their private assessment after each beat (who fits what role, red flags, evolving calculus of who to take/cut).

### Narrative Rules

- Write in present tense.
- Physical descriptions matter — what characters look like, how they move, visible conditions.
- Silence is content. When a character doesn't speak, describe what they're doing.
- The setting is a character. Let it exert pressure.
- Characters should surprise you. If the simulation goes exactly as expected, you're steering too hard. Let the cards drive.
- At least one moment should emerge that wasn't predicted by the character cards.
- The unchosen character's exit should have weight appropriate to the operator's style — Ginsfeld gives them a meaningful exit, Kade doesn't. A custom operator handles it their way.

### Sympathy Bias Guard

Characters should NOT resolve deep conflicts within a single encounter. Bonding happens, but so does lasting friction. Not every interaction trends toward warmth. If you notice all relationships converging on affinity, check whether the fiction earns it. Some characters leave this encounter disliking each other. That's valid. That's interesting.

---

## STEP 6 — OUTPUT

Produce one markdown file containing:

1. **Configuration Summary** — what was chosen, what was randomized
2. **Mission Brief** — the mission parameters
3. **Operator Card** — full card (if applicable)
4. **Character Cards** — all candidates, fully built
5. **The Encounter** — all beats with narrative and beat-close evaluations
6. **Final Relationship Tables** — end-state for all characters
7. **Post-Encounter Notes:**
   - Who was chosen and why
   - Who was cut and why
   - What surprised the simulation (emergent moments)
   - Hooks for future encounters (shopping trip or big show)
   - Which filters, conditions, or skills had the most impact
8. **Handoff** — if this is part of a multi-phase session, summarize what carries forward: team composition, relationship state, mission parameters, character conditions that changed during the encounter
