# Settings

Pre-built environments for session phases. Choose or roll, or generate fresh.

---

## Recruiting Settings

| # | Setting | Tone |
|---|---------|------|
| 1 | Basement beneath a library. Fluorescent lights, folding chairs, bad coffee. Institutional intimacy. | Claustrophobic, sincere |
| 2 | A Zoom call / livestream. Five faces in a grid, chat scrolling, viewer count flickering. | Performative, surveilled |
| 3 | A used bookshop after hours. Dust, amber light, narrow aisles, the smell of old paper. | Warm, conspiratorial |
| 4 | A train car on a route that doesn't appear on any map. The landscape outside doesn't repeat but doesn't progress. | Liminal, dreamlike |
| 5 | A waiting room. Plastic chairs, old magazines, a number display that hasn't changed in hours. No one knows what they're waiting for. | Kafkaesque, anxious |
| 6 | A rooftop at dusk. A city that looks familiar but isn't. The operator arrived first and set up a table with drinks. | Open, exposed, deceptively casual |

## Shopping Trip Settings

| # | Setting | Tone |
|---|---------|------|
| 1 | A night market that exists between texts. Stalls selling artifacts, rumors, and things that might be cursed. | Chaotic, sensory overload |
| 2 | The Bindery's workshop. Quiet, precise, every surface covered in tools and half-repaired objects. | Reverent, technical |
| 3 | A safehouse maintained by the Dockhands. Sparse, functional, walls covered in transit maps between texts. | Utilitarian, transient |
| 4 | An abandoned theater. The stage is set for a play that was never performed. Props are real artifacts. | Theatrical, melancholy |

## Big Show Settings

These are typically determined by the target book, but the approach vector and staging area can be chosen:

| # | Approach | Tone |
|---|----------|------|
| 1 | Through the front — entering the text as it wants to be read, following the narrative in. | Immersive, dangerous |
| 2 | Through the margins — slipping in through annotations, footnotes, and editorial apparatus. | Sneaky, fragile |
| 3 | Through a connection — entering via a linked text and crossing over at a point of shared reference. | Indirect, unpredictable |
| 4 | Through a wound — a place where the text has been damaged, redacted, or corrupted. The gap is the door. | Violent, unstable |
