Iteration 3

Mission Brief
Target (Roll: 3): A character trapped inside a book who is trying to escape into the wrong story. The character is a young soldier named Andrei — a minor figure from a sprawling war epic — who has realized he is fictional and is attempting to cross into a pastoral romance two shelves over. He believes that if he can enter the love story, he won't have to die in Chapter 41. He's wrong — the pastoral romance is a closed text, and forcing entry will collapse both narratives.
Opposition (Roll: 1): A rival bookhunter named Lisle Kade — faster, better funded, and operating without ethical constraints. Kade doesn't want to save Andrei or stabilize the texts. She wants to weaponize the breach point — a character crossing between texts creates a momentary structural vulnerability that Kade can exploit to extract rare textual material from both books simultaneously. She has a three-person team already in position.
Complication (Roll: 6): The target text — the war epic — is emotionally resonant to one of the team members. It will recognize their affinity and try to keep them, offering them a role in its narrative, a place that feels like belonging. The text isn't malicious. It's lonely. It's losing a character and it wants a replacement.
Ginsfeld Knows (Roll: 1): Everything about the target — Andrei's location, his motivation, the structural damage his escape attempt is causing, the timeline before collapse. Almost nothing about the opposition — he knows Kade exists, knows she's in the field, but doesn't know her team composition, her timeline, or her extraction method.

Method: Group therapy as audition
What He's Looking For

1. The Persuader — someone who can reach Andrei, talk him down from his escape attempt, or convince him there's another way. Needs narrative intelligence, empathy, and the ability to enter someone else's story without breaking it.

2. The Shield — someone who can handle Kade's team when they arrive. Physical capability, judgment under fire, willingness to stand ground. Not just muscle — the war epic's emotional pull means the Shield needs to be someone who can resist the text's seduction.

3. The Operator — someone who can manage the tactical situation — Kade's team, the structural instability, the timeline. Needs to think three moves ahead and act without hesitation when the plan breaks.

Decision Criteria

Competence, emotional stability under pressure, resistance to the complication. The war epic will try to keep someone — Ginsfeld needs to identify who's most vulnerable and either cut them or position them where the text can't reach. Wildcard tolerance: low. This mission has too many unknowns about Kade to afford an unpredictable team member.

Beat 1 The Room Before

Ginsfeld arrives early. He sets out five folding chairs in the basement beneath the Metro Library, tests the coffee urn, adjusts the hand-lettered sign that reads Wednesday Support Group — All Welcome. The fluorescent light buzzes its familiar rhythm. He sits in his chair and opens his notebook.

He reviews what he knows. Andrei — young soldier, war epic, minor figure, Chapter 41 death sentence. The boy has realized he is fictional. He is trying to cross into a pastoral romance two shelves over. He believes the love story will save him. He is wrong. The pastoral romance is a closed text. Forcing entry will collapse both narratives.

Then there is Kade. Lisle Kade. He knows the name. He knows she's faster, better funded, and that she operates without ethical constraints. He knows she has a three-person team already in position. He does not know her timeline, her method, or her team composition. He knows she wants to weaponize the breach point — the moment Andrei crosses, both texts become structurally vulnerable, and Kade can extract rare material from both simultaneously.

He needs speed, persuasion, and someone who can handle a hostile operator. He needs three people out of the four who are coming tonight. He needs to figure out which three before the coffee gets cold.

Ginsfeld's Assessment
Setup. Four chairs, four candidates, one cut. I know the target inside and out. I know almost nothing about Kade. That asymmetry is dangerous. I need a Persuader who can reach Andrei, a Shield who can handle Kade's team, and an Operator who can manage the tactical picture. The complication — the war epic trying to absorb a team member — means I also need to identify who's most vulnerable to emotional resonance and either cut them or shield them from it.
