# THE WEDNESDAY GROUP — Iteration 1

---

## MISSION BRIEF

**Target:** A character trapped inside a book who is trying to escape into the wrong story. The character is pushing against the seams of their text, and every failed escape attempt destabilizes the surrounding narratives. If they break through into the wrong story, the cross-contamination could corrupt both texts permanently.

**Opposition:** The Redactors. They've flagged the target for erasure rather than recovery. Their position: a character trying to escape is a character who has gone wrong, and wrong characters don't get rescued — they get unmade. The Redactors have a three-person strike team already in the field.

**Complication:** One member of the team will be recognized by inhabitants of the target text. Whoever it is will draw attention, suspicion, and potentially hostile response. The team won't know who until they're already inside.

**Ginsfeld Knows:** Everything about the opposition — the Redactors' methods, their team composition, their timeline (72 hours before they begin erasure). Almost nothing about the target — not which book, not which character, not why they're trying to escape. His contact gave him the job and the opposition brief. The rest is fog.

## THE ENCOUNTER

### BEAT 1 — Sei Arrives

The basement smells like wet concrete and instant coffee. The fluorescent tube nearest the door has committed to a rhythm: buzz-flicker-buzz-flicker, every four seconds. Ginsfeld counts the intervals while he straightens the folding chairs into something approaching a circle. Five chairs. The fifth is his.

The door opens at 8:47 PM — thirteen minutes early. Sei Shōnagon descends the stairs with the posture of someone entering a throne room and the expression of someone cataloguing its inadequacies. She wears layered robes — plum over ash over white — that belong to no century currently in use. Her hair is drawn up and pinned with what appears to be an actual writing brush. She pauses three steps from the bottom and surveys the room.

Ginsfeld watches her eyes move. Left to right. The coffee urn (dismissed). The folding chairs (noted). The hand-lettered sign (read twice). The flickering light (categorized as either *Things That Are Unpleasant* or *Things That Reveal the Poverty of the Host*). Him.

"Dr. Ginsfeld." She says it the way you'd say the name of a restaurant you've read mixed reviews about. "I am Sei Shōnagon. I was told this group addresses the condition of displacement."

"It does," Ginsfeld says. He gestures toward the chairs. "Coffee?"

She looks at the Styrofoam cups. "No," she says, and sits in the chair that gives her a sightline to both the door and the rest of the circle. She chose it in under two seconds.

**[PASSIVE SKILL TRIGGER: Aesthetic Resonance]** — Sei enters a new space.
*Roll: d20(11) + WIS(+2) = 13. Medium success.*
Sei gets a strong impression of the room: discordant, deliberately understated, a space that is pretending to be less than it is. The shabbiness reads as costume, not poverty. She files this away. The man is performing humility. Why?

> **Beat-Close Relationships:**
> - **Ginsfeld → Sei:** Affinity +2 (she's early, observant, chose the strategic chair). *She's already reading the room. That's either very useful or very dangerous.*
> - **Sei → Ginsfeld:** Aversion +1 (the setting is beneath her; he's performing). *A man who arranges folding chairs with this much care is arranging something else.*

*Ginsfeld's Assessment: Smart. Dangerously smart. She'll figure out what this is before the coffee gets cold. I need to decide early whether that's an asset or a problem.*

### BEAT 2 — Yvain Arrives

At 8:53 PM the door opens with more force than necessary and a man fills the stairway. Yvain has to duck — the lintel was built for librarians, not knights, and he catches it with his shoulder anyway. He descends carefully, one hand on the railing, his body angled sideways in the narrow space. He is large in the way that stone walls are large: not aggressive, just unavoidably present.

He wears a tunic and trousers that look hand-mended, boots that have seen forest floors, and a sword belt with no sword. The empty scabbard hangs at his left hip like a question. His face is weathered, bearded, early middle age — handsome once, maybe, before something stripped the softness out. His eyes scan the room the instant he clears the stairs: door, far wall, Ginsfeld, the woman, back to the door.

**[PASSIVE SKILL TRIGGER: Forest Sense]** — Yvain enters an enclosed, unfamiliar space.
*Roll: d20(14) + WIS(+2) = 16. Medium-high success.*
He catalogs: one exit (the stairs), a utility door behind the coffee table (locked, probably storage), a ventilation grate near the ceiling (too small). Four folding chairs plus one occupied. The woman in robes is watching him. The air down here is still. No draft from the grate. He notes this and files it under *enclosed, single egress, manageable.*

"Welcome," Ginsfeld says. "I'm Donald. You must be —"

"Yvain." He says it like a stone dropping into water. He looks at the chairs, selects the one nearest the stairs, and sits. The chair groans.

Sei watches him settle. Her fingers move against her thigh — writing invisible notes. A knight without a sword. An empty scabbard worn anyway, which means the absence is a wound, not a choice. His eyes went to the exits before they went to the people. She adds him to a list she hasn't titled yet.

Yvain feels her looking. He meets her eyes for a moment — not challenging, just acknowledging — and then looks away. Something about her gaze makes the air feel thinner.

**[FILTER: The Oath That Echoes — passive presence activation]**
The room's moral gravity shifts slightly. Ginsfeld, mid-sentence about the coffee, feels a brief, unwelcome prickle at the base of his skull — the somatic signature of a man who lies for a living sitting near a man whose mere presence makes lying more expensive.

> **Beat-Close Relationships:**
> - **Ginsfeld → Yvain:** Affinity +1 (physical presence, reliability read). *Built like a battlement. The empty scabbard means he follows rules — even stupid ones. That's useful.*
> - **Yvain → Ginsfeld:** Affinity +1 (appreciates the welcome, the simplicity). *He offered coffee before he asked my name. A small courtesy. I notice these.*
> - **Sei → Yvain:** Affinity +1 (recognizes honor), Aversion +1 (crude, forest-smell, no appreciation of the aesthetic). *An honest man. You can see it in the way he wears his guilt on his belt. But he smells like pine sap and hasn't considered the line of his tunic once.*
> - **Yvain → Sei:** Aversion +1 (felt the judgment). *The woman in the robes. She looked at me the way my lord's clerk looked at supply ledgers. I am a line item.*

*Ginsfeld's Assessment: The Shield. Almost certainly. I just need to know if the madness I can see behind his eyes is dormant or a lit fuse.*

### BEAT 3 — Azaro Arrives

"I never rest," Azaro says. "But I'll eat."

"I'll have food."

Sei and Fevvers stand by the stairs, not quite together but not apart. Sei reaches into her robes and produces a small piece of paper — torn from what, it's unclear — and writes something on it with the brush from her hair. She holds it out to Fevvers.

Fevvers takes it. It's a single line of calligraphy, Japanese characters brushed with a steady hand. "What's it say?"

"'Things That Are Near Though Distant.' It is a list category from my book. It means: things that seem far away but are closer than you think. It is what you are."

Fevvers looks at the paper for a long time. Then she folds it carefully and puts it inside her coat, near the heart.

"Right," Fevvers says. Her voice is thick. "Right, then."

Yvain is the last to leave. He pauses at the bottom of the stairs and looks back at Ginsfeld.

"If the boy is harmed," he says. It is not a complete sentence. It does not need to be.

**[FILTER: The Oath That Echoes — Yvain issues an implicit promise/threat]**
The weight of the unfinished sentence fills the stairwell. Ginsfeld understands it perfectly: this is the knight's version of a binding contract. If Azaro is hurt, Yvain will not ask questions. He will not wait for explanations. He will act with the full force of a man who went mad in a forest and came back with a lion.

"Understood," Ginsfeld says.

Yvain turns and walks up the stairs. His footsteps shake the fluorescent fixture.

Ginsfeld stands alone in the basement. The coffee urn gurgles. The grey spirit shape near the ceiling tilts its head at him — he can't see it, but he feels something watching. Through the northeast wall, very faint, something beats against the glass between stories.

He sits back down in his folding chair, pulls out a notebook, and begins to plan.

> **Final Beat Relationships:**
> - **Sei → Fevvers:** Affinity +1 (the calligraphy — an act of connection from a woman who connects to nothing). *I gave her a piece of my book. I do not know why. I have never done that. She is near though distant. I will stand at the door's edge and make certain she is safe when they return.*

## POST-ENCOUNTER NOTES

### Team Composition

**Inside team:** Azaro (Navigator), Yvain (Shield), Sei Shōnagon (Face)
**Entry point:** Fevvers (Door)
**Handler:** Ginsfeld (external)

Ginsfeld broke his own three-person constraint. The mission briefing specified a crew of three, but the encounter dynamics produced a four-person deployment with the fourth (Fevvers) positioned at the entry point rather than inside the target text. This was driven by two factors:

1. Azaro's identification of Fevvers' filter as a tactical asset for obscuring the entry point from the Redactors — a use case nobody else, including Ginsfeld, had considered.
2. Yvain's intervention in Beat 16, which flipped the Sei/Fevvers assessment by highlighting Fevvers' conspicuousness and Sei's stealth.

### Who Was Chosen and Why

- **Azaro** — confirmed as Navigator in Beat 8 when he described his ability to see narrative seams, and again in Beat 12 when he independently sensed the mission target through the basement wall. He is irreplaceable.
- **Yvain** — the Shield from the start. His physical capability, protective instinct (especially toward Azaro), and the Oath That Echoes filter (which extracts genuine commitments from those around him) make him the team's moral and physical anchor.
- **Sei Shōnagon** — initially set to be cut, she was restored by Yvain's advocacy in Beat 16. Her anonymity (the Marginalia haven't identified her despite months of annotation), her observational precision, and her ability to navigate complex social environments make her the ideal Face for moving unseen inside an unknown text.
- **Fevvers** — not cut, but repositioned. Her Suspension of Disbelief filter, originally valued as an asset inside the text, was reframed by Azaro as an entry-point asset: she can make the door uncertain, preventing the Redactors from locating it. Her broken wing makes interior deployment risky; the Door role accommodates the injury while maximizing her unique capability.

### Who Was Cut and Why

**Nobody.** This is the first iteration in which the four-to-three cut did not result in elimination. The simulation produced a scenario where all four characters found distinct, necessary roles. The engine's constraint (crew of 3) was bent rather than broken — three inside, one at the threshold.

This outcome was driven entirely by character interaction, not by design. Sei's self-sacrifice in Beat 15 set up Yvain's counter-argument in Beat 16, which set up Azaro's tactical reframe in Beat 17. Each character's contribution was necessary to reach the final configuration. If any one of them had behaved differently — if Sei hadn't advocated for Fevvers, if Yvain hadn't advocated for Sei, if Azaro hadn't reimagined Fevvers' role — the cut would have proceeded as expected.

### What Surprised the Simulation

1. **The Sei/Fevvers exchange in Beats 9 and 15.** The initial hostility between the Heian court lady and the Cockney aerialiste was expected. The mutual recognition was not. Sei's "near though distant" calligraphy in Beat 20 — a genuine act of connection from a character designed for clinical observation — was emergent and unplanned.

2. **Yvain's tactical advocacy in Beat 16.** The character's Low Game Savvy suggested he would not engage in strategic analysis. But the simulation distinguished between social Game Savvy (reading hidden agendas — low) and battlefield assessment (evaluating tactical deployment — high). Yvain's intervention was the pivot point of the entire encounter.

3. **Azaro's "bird hitting glass" in Beat 12.** The spirit child independently confirmed the mission target through sheer perceptual power, which was mechanically predicted (Between the Pages, critical success) but narratively striking. The metaphor — a bird hitting glass — became the mission's emotional core, giving the abstract target a visceral reality.

4. **Nobody was cut.** The engine specifies four becomes three. The characters found a way to be four. This felt honest — forcing a cut when all four characters had found distinct roles would have been artificial steering, which the engine explicitly warns against.

5. **The Oath That Echoes as truth serum.** Yvain's filter had an outsized effect on Ginsfeld, forcing genuine commitments from a character whose defining trait is deception. The promise to return (Beat 18) and the acceptance of Yvain's threat regarding Azaro (Beat 20) were both moments where Ginsfeld's Low Honesty was overridden by the filter's moral gravity. This will have consequences on the mission.
