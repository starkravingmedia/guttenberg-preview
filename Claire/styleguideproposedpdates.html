Proposed Updates

Style Guide Additions

New components needed to render the Ginsfeld Sessions. Each is designed to extend the existing design system without breaking its conventions.

✶ ✶ ✶

Mission Brief Panel

Structured intelligence briefing with double-border frame

Mission Brief
Target: A character trapped inside a book who is trying to escape into the wrong story.
Opposition: The Redactors. Three-person strike team. 72-hour window.
Complication: One member of the team will be recognized by inhabitants of the target text.
HTML
<div class="mission-brief">
  <div class="mission-brief-title">Mission Brief</div>
  <div class="mission-field">
    <span class="mission-field-label">Target: </span>
    <span class="mission-field-value">Description...</span>
  </div>
</div>

Double-border frame echoes .parchment-bg. Labels are --blood in Cinzel uppercase. Reuses the ::before inner-border pattern from the page structure.

Condition Block

Persistent states with duration, visibility, and observability metadata

The List-Maker's Compulsion
Permanent Semi-Private o2o
She categorizes everything she encounters — people, rooms, feelings, the quality of light. Others notice her eyes moving, cataloguing. Her fingers twitch as though writing.
HTML
<div class="condition">
  <div class="condition-name">Name</div>
  <div class="condition-meta">
    <span class="condition-tag condition-tag--permanent">Permanent</span>
    <span class="condition-tag">Semi-Private</span>
    <span class="condition-tag">o2o</span>
  </div>
  <div class="condition-desc">Description...</div>
</div>

Left-border accent, parchment-wash background. Metadata tags are badge-like (Cinzel uppercase). --permanent modifier uses --blood border. --private uses dashed border. Follows the badge convention established in the styleguide.

Skill Block

Roll skills with trigger, mechanic, ranges, and cost

Devastating Observation Active
Trigger: Sei chooses to study a person or situation closely and deliver her assessment
d20 + CHA (+3)
18+ She identifies not just what someone is hiding, but WHY — the emotional architecture beneath the mask.
10–17 She reads the surface accurately — posture, tells, inconsistencies.
1–9 She projects her own biases onto the target and misreads them.
Cost: The target always knows they've been observed. High-success observations leave the target feeling exposed.
HTML
<div class="skill-block">
  <div class="skill-header">
    <span class="skill-name">Name</span>
    <span class="skill-type skill-type--active">Active</span>
  </div>
  <div class="skill-trigger">Trigger: ...</div>
  <div class="skill-mechanic">d20 + STAT (+mod)</div>
  <div class="skill-range">
    <span class="skill-range-label skill-range-label--high">18+</span>
    High success description.
  </div>
  <div class="skill-range">
    <span class="skill-range-label">10&ndash;17</span>
    Medium success description.
  </div>
  <div class="skill-range">
    <span class="skill-range-label skill-range-label--low">1&ndash;9</span>
    Failure description.
  </div>
  <div class="skill-cost">
    <span class="skill-cost-label">Cost: </span>
    Always-paid cost.
  </div>
</div>

Active/Passive type badges follow the .badge convention. Active uses --blood, Passive uses --gold. Range labels use gold (high), ink (medium), blood (low). Cost is italicized with a blood-colored label. Borrows the bordered panel from .team-member.

Aura Block

Passive aura that changes the social environment

"Things That Lose by Being Painted"
The world presents itself to Sei in hierarchies of elegance. When she enters a room, everyone present becomes subtly aware of whether they are being found pleasing or wanting.
HTML
<div class="aura-block">
  <div class="aura-name">"Filter Name"</div>
  <div class="aura-desc">Description...</div>
</div>

Gold border and centered text distinguish filters from conditions and skills. The ~ symbol in the ::before pseudo-element suggests a wave or aura. Italic EB Garamond for the dreamy, always-on quality of filters.

Dice Roll (Inline)

Skill triggers within the narrative flow

Passive Skill Trigger: Aesthetic Resonance Roll: d20(11) + WIS(+2) = 13. Medium success.
HTML
<div class="dice-roll">
  <span class="dice-roll-label">Passive Skill Trigger: Name</span>
  <span class="dice-roll-skill">Roll:</span> d20(11) + WIS(+2) =
  <span class="dice-roll-result">13</span>.
  <span class="dice-roll-outcome">Medium success.</span>
</div>

Blood left-border distinguishes mechanical interventions from narrative. The label is Cinzel uppercase in blood. Result is Cinzel bold. Outcome is italic. Mirrors the left-border accent pattern of conditions and chains.

Aura activations within the narrative flow

Aura: The Oath That Echoes The room's moral gravity shifts slightly. Ginsfeld feels a brief, unwelcome prickle at the base of his skull.
HTML
<div class="aura-trigger">

Gold left-border (vs. blood for dice rolls). Same structural pattern as the dice roll block, differentiated by color. The gold signals passive/environmental effects while blood signals active mechanical interventions.

Relationship Deltas

Beat-close affinity and aversion changes with internal thoughts

Beat-Close Relationships
Ginsfeld → Sei: Affinity +2 — She's early, observant, chose the strategic chair. She's already reading the room.
Sei → Ginsfeld: Aversion +1 — A man who arranges folding chairs with this much care is arranging something else.
HTML
<div class="relationships-block">
  <div class="relationships-label">Beat-Close Relationships</div>
  <div class="rel-delta">
    <span class="rel-pair">Ginsfeld &rarr; Sei:</span>
    <span class="rel-change--positive">Affinity +2</span>
    <span class="rel-thought">&mdash; Internal thought.</span>
  </div>
</div>

Dashed border and parchment-wash background echo the .sidelined-block pattern — relationships are metadata that sit alongside the narrative rather than in it. Gold for positive changes, blood for negative. Thoughts are italic EB Garamond.

Assessment Block

Ginsfeld's internal monologue at the end of each beat

Ginsfeld's Assessment
Smart. Dangerously smart. She'll figure out what this is before the coffee gets cold. I need to decide early whether that's an asset or a problem.
HTML
<div class="assessment">
  <div class="assessment-label">Ginsfeld's Assessment</div>
  Italic internal monologue text.
</div>

Ink left-border (darker than conditions or filters) signals a different voice — the handler's private calculus. Italic EB Garamond throughout. The darker border distinguishes it from the gold (filters) and blood (dice) accent blocks.

Cumulative affinity/aversion summary

<table class="rel-table">

    <tr><th>Character</th><th>Affinity</th>
    <th>Aversion</th><th>Net</th><th>Summary</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr>
      <td>Name</td><td>10</td><td>3</td>
      <td class="net-positive">+7</td>
      <td>Summary text.</td>
    </tr>
  </tbody>

Cinzel headers, EB Garamond body. Net score colored: .net-positive (gold), .net-negative (blood), .net-neutral (ink-light). Character names are Cinzel bold. Summary column is italic. Follows standard table styling with ink header border and parchment-dark row borders.

Impact Table

Filters, conditions, and skills rated by impact level

Element Impact Notes
The Oath That Echoes Critical Shaped Ginsfeld's behavior throughout; forced genuine commitments.
Forest Sense Low–Medium Provided tactical awareness but no major narrative impact.
HTML
<table class="impact-table">
  <tr>
    <td>Name</td>
    <td><span class="impact-critical">Critical</span></td>
    <td>Notes...</td>
  </tr>
</table>

Impact levels: .impact-critical (blood), .impact-high (gold), .impact-medium (ink-light), .impact-low (ink-light at 0.6 opacity). All in Cinzel uppercase. Follows the same semantic color coding used throughout the system: blood for danger/urgency, gold for significance, fading for diminishing impact.

Post-Encounter Note

Analysis blocks for design commentary

What Surprised the Simulation
  1. The Sei/Fevvers exchange in Beats 9 and 15. The initial hostility was expected. The mutual recognition was not.
  2. Yvain's tactical advocacy in Beat 16. His Low Game Savvy suggested he would not engage in strategic analysis.
HTML
<div class="post-note">
  <div class="post-note-title">Title</div>
  <div class="post-note-text">
    Content with lists, paragraphs, etc.
  </div>
</div>

A neutral analytical container. Cinzel title, EB Garamond body. Parchment-dark border and subtle parchment-wash background. Suitable for design commentary, session notes, and meta-analysis sections that step outside the narrative voice.
