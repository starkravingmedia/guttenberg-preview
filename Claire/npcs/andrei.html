A young soldier, war epic, minor figure
Andrei
NPC — Mission Target
A young soldier trapped inside a war epic who has realized he is fictional. Minor figure, Chapter 41 death sentence. He is trying to cross into a pastoral romance two shelves over because he believes the love story will save him. He is wrong — the pastoral romance is a closed text, and forcing entry will collapse both narratives. But he doesn’t know that. He only knows he doesn’t want to die in someone else’s story.

Chapter 41
Permanent Private no2o
He knows exactly when he dies. Chapter 41, paragraph three, a stray shell during a retreat that the narrative doesn’t even bother to dramatize. A minor figure’s minor death. The knowledge sits behind his eyes like a countdown.
Fictional Awareness
Permanent Semi-private no2o
He knows he is a character in a book. He can feel the edges of the page, the weight of the binding, the author’s indifference. Most characters never realize. Andrei did. It broke something in him and opened something else.
The Pastoral Door
Until resolved Public o2o
He has found what he believes is a crossing point — a seam between his war epic and a pastoral romance two shelves over. He can feel the other text’s warmth, its gentleness. He is wrong about what will happen if he crosses. He is not wrong about the seam.
A Soldier’s Hands
Permanent Public o2o
Young, but already shaped by the text he inhabits. His hands shake sometimes — not from cowardice, but from the effort of holding still in a narrative that keeps pushing him toward violence.

The Weight of Minor Characters
In Andrei’s presence, everyone remembers a character they skipped over — a face in a crowd scene, a name mentioned once and never again, a soldier who died offscreen. The memory is specific and arrives with an unexpected pang of guilt. Minor characters, Andrei’s proximity suggests, were always real. You just didn’t notice.
