Lady Macbeth
Actor — Unaffiliated
Queen without a kingdom. The woman who held the knife's handle while her husband held the blade. She washed her hands once and the water ran red and she has been washing them ever since. She does not regret what she did. She regrets that it wasn't enough.

The Spot
Permanent Semi-private o2o
She washes her hands. Constantly. Not dramatically — quietly, compulsively, rubbing thumb against palm in a motion so practiced it's nearly invisible. But in still moments, in pauses between words, her hands move. People notice. They don't always know what they're seeing.
Crown Without a Head
Permanent Private no2o
She achieved everything. The crown, the throne, the power. Then it ate her alive and she died offstage, unmourned, reduced to a doctor's footnote. She carries the knowledge that ambition fulfilled is just emptiness with better furniture.
The Sleepwalker
3 encounters Semi-private no2o
Under extreme emotional pressure, she enters a dissociative state — eyes open, voice flat, speaking truths she would never say while conscious. She doesn't remember these episodes. Others do.
Eyes of the Court
Permanent Public o2o
She carries herself like royalty in exile. Straight spine, level gaze, the precise economy of movement that comes from years of being watched. Even in a basement, she commands the geometry of the room.
Reputations
"The Unsex'd"
Source: The Redactors
She once asked the darkness to strip her of compassion so she could act. The Redactors took notice. A character who voluntarily requests the removal of her own qualities is either the bravest or the most dangerous thing in any room. They watch her the way you watch a controlled burn.
"The Hand-Washer"
Source: Wide
Everyone who knows her story knows about the hands. It's the first thing people mention and the last thing she wants discussed.

Aura
The Sharpening
In Lady Macbeth's presence, people feel their ambitions clarify and their moral hesitations soften. Not disappear — soften. The edges of "I shouldn't" become "but what if I did?" The effect is subtle, persistent, and entirely unconscious on her part.
