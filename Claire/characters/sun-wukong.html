The Monkey King, in armor and circlet
Sun Wukong
Actor — Unaffiliated
The Monkey King. Born from a stone, crowned himself king of the monkeys, stole immortality three times over, challenged all of heaven, and lost — not to any god but to the Buddha's palm. Spent five hundred years under a mountain. Walked west with a monk as penance. He is the greatest warrior in Chinese literature and the worst student. He does not follow rules. He follows whoever earns the right to hold the leash, and even then he pulls.

Conditions
The Circlet
Permanent Public o2o
A golden band around his head, placed there by the Buddha as a leash. When someone speaks to him with genuine authority — not bluster, not volume, but the real thing — it tightens. He flinches at commands. On camera, it reads as a gold headband that catches the light at odd angles. The internet thinks it's a fashion choice.
Five Hundred Years
Permanent Private no2o
He spent five centuries under a mountain. Alone. In the dark. He doesn't talk about it. He doesn't talk about anything quietly. But small rooms make his voice louder than it needs to be, and locked doors make his hands move to the staff he no longer carries. Claustrophobia worn as volume.
Seventy-Two Transformations
Permanent Semi-private no2o
He can change form. In the Literary Web, transformation is unstable — his edges shimmer when he's emotional, his face flickers between human and something older. On Zoom, it reads as camera glitches. The audience blames his WiFi. It's not his WiFi.
Stone-Born
Permanent Public o2o
His skin has a faintly mineral quality — smoother than human, slightly luminous. On Zoom, it looks like unusually good lighting. His eyes are amber. The chat is obsessed with his skincare routine. He does not have a skincare routine. He was born from a rock.
Reputations
"The Great Sage Equal to Heaven"
Source: The Lamplighters
He challenged all of heaven and nearly won. The Lamplighters maintain a file on him that is three inches thick and still classified as "inconclusive." He is categorized as an extinction-level event that happens to currently be cooperative. The "currently" is underlined twice.
"The Penitent"
Source: The Ashkeepers
He walked west. He protected the monk. He carried the scriptures. The Ashkeepers, who tend dying texts, know the version of Wukong who earned his title through service. They trust him more than the Lamplighters do. They might be wrong.

The Iron Staff Active
Mechanic: d20 + STR (+4)
DC 10: Devastating physical force — he hits like a siege engine, and what he hits stays hit.
DC 15: The impact carries narrative weight — the target doesn't just take damage, they feel the weight of 500 years of containment behind the blow.
DC 20: Ruyi Jingu Bang manifests at full mythic scale. The staff doesn't just strike — it restructures the local narrative around the point of impact. Physics become mythology.
Cost: Using the staff at full power inside someone else's text is vandalism. The text fights back. Structural integrity drops. Every DC 20 roll in the field makes the extraction window shorter.
Aura
The Challenge Acknowledged
In Sun Wukong's presence, hierarchies become visible and contestable. Authority figures feel their authority noticed — not attacked, not undermined, but seen as a thing that exists and could, theoretically, be challenged. Subordinates feel permission to look up. The effect doesn't encourage rebellion. It encourages the awareness that rebellion is a verb that conjugates. The Monkey King bowed to one being in his entire existence, and it took a mountain on his back to teach him how.
