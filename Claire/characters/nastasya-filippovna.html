Beautiful and terrible
Nastasya Filippovna
Actor — Unaffiliated
The most beautiful woman in Petersburg and the most dangerous thing in any room she enters. Totsky took her childhood. Society called her ruined. She lit one hundred thousand roubles on fire to watch a man crawl and discovered that destruction was the only language anyone understood when she spoke it. Prince Myshkin offered to save her. She ran. She always runs from the thing she wants most.

Conditions
The Ruined Woman
Permanent Private no2o
Totsky took her when she was a child. She carries what was done to her like a blade pressed against her own skin. She doesn't speak about it directly. She doesn't need to. Every choice she makes — the destruction, the flight, the refusal of love — is a response to the first wound.
The Hundred Thousand Roubles
Permanent Semi-private o2o
She once threw a fortune into a fireplace and watched a man crawl to save it. The gesture defined her publicly: she would rather destroy what has value than let others reduce her to what she's worth. On camera, it manifests as a quality of contempt for material things.
Beautiful and Terrible
Permanent Public o2o
Her beauty is the kind that makes people stop talking. Not conventional beauty — the beauty of something that might burn you if you touch it. On Zoom, it reads as the face the camera was invented for, but there is something in her eyes that makes viewers look at the chat instead of at her. She is beautiful the way a fire is beautiful.
The Offer
3 encounters Private no2o
Prince Myshkin offered to marry her. The only person who ever looked at her and saw someone worth saving rather than someone worth owning. She ran. She runs from every offer of genuine kindness because accepting it would require believing she deserves it.
Reputations
"The Fireplace"
Source: The Auctioneers
She destroyed one hundred thousand roubles to prove a point about value. The Auctioneers, who measure value for a living, have debated the gesture at every annual meeting since. Half consider it the most courageous act in Russian literature. Half consider it insanity.
Roll Skills
The Confession Active
Mechanic: d20 + CHA (+4)
DC 10: She identifies one hidden motivation in the room — names it, plainly, without decoration.
DC 15: She strips the entire social dynamic to its bones — who wants what, who's lying about what, who's afraid of what — and says it aloud.
DC 20: The social fiction collapses. Every pretense in the room shatters simultaneously, and everyone stands exposed in the wreckage of what they were pretending.
Cost: Every confession includes her own truth, and her truth cuts deepest. At DC 15+, she reveals something about herself she didn't intend to share. At DC 20, the revelation is devastating.
The Fireplace Passive
Trigger: Fires when someone offers her something she genuinely wants, or treats her as if she has inherent value
Mechanic: d20 + WIS (+2)
DC 8: She flinches. The offer registers as a threat. She deflects with sarcasm.
DC 14: She begins to self-sabotage — says something designed to make the giver retract the offer.
DC 20: She destroys the offering. Spectacularly, publicly, irrevocably. Not because she doesn't want it — because she can't bear to have it.
Cost: The destruction is real. Whatever she destroys stays destroyed. And everyone who witnesses it understands, on some level, that they just watched someone set fire to their own salvation.
Aura
The Mirror of Worth
In Nastasya Filippovna's presence, people become aware of their own moral compromises. Not their grand sins — their quiet ones. The casual cruelties, the small cowardices, the moments they chose comfort over principle. The effect is specific and uncomfortable. She doesn't judge. She doesn't need to. Her presence makes self-judgment unavoidable, the way a mirror makes your face unavoidable.
